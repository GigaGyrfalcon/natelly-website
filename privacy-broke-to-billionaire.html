Privacy Policy

Broke to Billionaire

This privacy policy explains how information is stored, processed, used, and shared in connection with the Broke to Billionaire mobile game.

Policy scope

This page covers the current app release and the services used to operate it.

Questions

For privacy questions, reach out to info@natelly.com.

Effective date

March 30, 2026

Overview

Broke to Billionaire is a mobile game published by Natelly. The app uses on-device storage plus limited third-party services to support gameplay, rewarded advertising, and in-app purchases. This privacy policy explains what information is stored on your device, what information may be processed by our service providers, and how that information is used.

Information stored on your device

The app stores gameplay and preference data locally on your device so your progress can continue between sessions. This may include saved progress, in-game currency totals, upgrades, prestige progress, claimed rewards, premium unlock status, sound and haptics settings, timestamps such as last active time, and tutorial progress.

Anonymous account and backend records

The app creates an anonymous account through Supabase so purchases can be verified and restored. This does not require a username, password, or profile details from you, but it does create a persistent account identifier and session data. We also store server-side entitlement and purchase verification records such as your anonymous user ID, purchased product IDs, granted premium features, original transaction IDs, purchase environment, related verification timestamps, and purchase audit logs.

Rewarded ads

The app uses Google AdMob to show rewarded ads. When ads are requested or displayed, Google and its partners may process information such as device and advertising identifiers, IP address, app and ad interaction data, diagnostics, and performance data in accordance with their own privacy terms. Rewarded ads are optional, but some in-game rewards are tied to watching an ad unless you have purchased the remove-ads feature.

In-app purchases

The app offers in-app purchases handled by Apple through the App Store. We do not receive your full payment card or billing information. To unlock and restore purchased features, the app receives transaction information from Apple, sends the signed transaction to our Supabase verification service, and stores purchase entitlement records needed to confirm ownership, restore purchases, prevent duplicate grants, and maintain an audit trail.

How information is used

Information handled by the app is used to save your progress, remember your settings, deliver rewarded ad benefits, process and restore purchases, prevent fraud or abuse in purchase verification, troubleshoot service issues, and comply with legal or platform requirements.

Sharing with service providers

We share limited information with service providers only as needed to operate the app features described above. These providers currently include Supabase for anonymous authentication and purchase entitlement storage, Google AdMob for rewarded advertising, and Apple for App Store purchase processing and restoration. Each provider handles information under its own terms and privacy practices.

Tracking and personalized advertising

On iOS, the app includes advertising-related tracking language required by Apple for personalized ads. Where platform rules require consent, tracking-related permissions or prompts may be shown by the operating system or advertising providers. If you deny tracking permission, ads may still be shown but may be less personalized.

Data retention and deletion

Locally stored game data remains on your device until you clear it in the app or uninstall the app. Anonymous session data may remain on the device until it expires or is removed. Server-side purchase and entitlement records may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to support purchase restoration, fraud prevention, dispute handling, recordkeeping, and legal compliance.

Children

The app is not intended to knowingly collect personal information from children without appropriate authorization. If you believe a child has provided information to us in a way that raises a privacy concern, please contact us so we can review the issue.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time as the app, its services, or legal requirements change. When we do, we will update the effective date shown on this page.

Your questions

If you have questions about this privacy policy or would like to contact Natelly about Broke to Billionaire, email info@natelly.com.
